The Concept02 / 09
The new paradigm

What is Vibe Coding?

Vibe coding is building software by describing what you want in natural language and letting an AI agent write the code for you.

You don't write syntax. You don't debug line by line. You describe your vision, and the AI translates it into a working application.

The term comes from Andrej Karpathy — the idea that you "give in to the vibes" and let the AI handle the implementation.

In practice

You write one detailed prompt describing the app you want. The AI builds it. Here's a real example →

Real Example — One Prompt, One App
Pimp'd — iOS Medical Education App
Full Conversation Transcript

I want to make an app for third-year medical students who are on their rotations. The purpose of this app is to allow students a tool to learn concepts that come up during the day while on rotations.

For context, students are on rounds with attendings and residents and often get asked questions that they may or may not know the answer to. The goal is to allow the student to make note of the topic and review it later.

For this app, I would like the user to hit their action button on the iPhone and talk into their microphone and say a topic or key point that they need to review later. The app will then take this topic and query the most up-to-date PubMed articles and feed it through ChatGPT to create a summary.

Additionally: 5 practice questions per topic, 5 concise Anki cards for each topic, and a 20-question weekly exam incorporating all topics reviewed.

Make this app sage green, light brown, tan, and other earthly colors with a clean user interface. The title of this app will be called "Pimp'd".

One prompt. One app. — Live demo to follow.

The Baby Analogy03 / 09
How learning works

The Neuron — Nature's Learning Machine

Hebb's Rule (1949)

"Neurons that fire together, wire together."

When two neurons activate simultaneously and repeatedly, the synapse between them strengthens. This is the biological basis for all learning — and the principle that artificial neural networks replicate through backpropagation.

Your neurons form connections called synapses. When you learn, certain synaptic pathways strengthen through repetition — a process called long-term potentiation (LTP). A baby's brain starts with random synaptic weights, then strengthens the right pathways and prunes the wrong ones through constant data. AI works the same way.

Scaling Up05 / 09
Words as Numbers

Embedding Matrices

Every word gets converted into a list of numbers — a vector. These vectors capture meaning, context, relationships. Similar words live near each other in this space.

Attention

Transformers

Transformers look at all words simultaneously and learn which ones matter most for predicting the next. This "attention" mechanism is what makes modern AI understand language.

Same principle as digit recognition — just billions of parameters, trained on the entire internet.

The Takeaway06 / 09
Why does this matter?

AI is not human.

It's glorified statistics. It will confidently give wrong answers, lose context, and make things up. Understanding this is what separates effective builders from frustrated ones.

The people who build amazing things with AI aren't treating it like a magic oracle. They're treating it like what it is: a powerful statistical tool that needs to be directed properly.

The Curse07 / 09
The other side

The Curse of Vibe

That example looked magical. But here's the reality: vibe coding without understanding is a trap.

When it fails

You're stuck. You don't know what went wrong. You don't know how to fix it. You keep throwing more prompts at it, and the codebase becomes an incomprehensible mess.

The solution

Understand the principles. Be specific. Own the problem. Iterate aggressively. Provide the right context. That's what turns the curse into a superpower.

The goal of this workshop is to make sure you don't fall into the trap.

The Principles08 / 09
Vibe Coding Principles
1

Be as specific as possible

The more precise your instructions, the narrower the output space. Vague prompts produce vague results.

2

Understand your problem better than AI

You don't need to write code. But you must know what you're building and why. Be the architect — AI handles implementation.

3

Iterate, iterate, iterate

Building with AI is not one-shot. Prompt, review, refine, correct, repeat. You are the loss function.

4

Context is everything

Give AI the right context — not all of it. Too little and it guesses blindly. Too much and the signal gets lost in noise.
